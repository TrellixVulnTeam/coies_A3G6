16                     AND  OTHER  R ELIEF AGAINST A NDREW  D. FELDMAN

17        The Court finds that Plaintiff, Securities and Exchange Commission (“Commission”), has 
18  filed a First Amended Complaint; and Defendant Andrew D. Feldman has entered a general 
19  appearance, consented to the Court’s jurisdiction over him personally and over the subject matter 
20  of this action, consented to the entry of this Order of Permanent Injunction and Other Relief 
21  against Andrew D. Feldman (referred to as “Order of Permanent Injunction”) without admitting 
22  or denying the allegations of the First Amended Complaint (except as to jurisdiction), waived 
23  findings of fact and conclusions of law, and waived any right to appeal from this Order of 
24  Permanent Injunction. 
25                                             I. 
26        IT IS ORDERED, ADJUDGED AND DECREED, that Andrew D. Feldman and his 
27  agents, servants, employees, attorneys, and all persons in active concert or participation with him 
28  who receive actual notice of this Order of Permanent Injunction by personal service or otherwise

 2  the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 (the “Exchange Act”) [15 U.S.C. § 78j(b)] and Rule 10b-5 
 3  promulgated thereunder [17 C.F.R. § 240.10b-5], by using any means or instrumentality of 
 4  interstate commerce, or of the mails, or of any facility of any national securities exchange, in 
 5  connection with the purchase or sale of any security: 
 6         (a) 	   to employ any device, scheme, or artifice to defraud;

 8                 necessary in order to make the statements made, in the light of the circumstances  
 9                 under which they were made, not misleading; or 
10         (c)	    to engage in any act, practice, or course of business which operates or would 
11                 operate as a fraud or deceit upon any person. 
12                                                 II. 
13         IT IS FURTHER ORDERED, ADJUDGED, AND DECREED that Andrew D. Feldman 
14  and his agents, servants, employees, attorneys, and all persons in active concert or participation 
15  with him who receive actual notice of this Order of Permanent Injunction by personal service or 
16  otherwise are permanently restrained and enjoined from violating, directly or indirectly, Section 
17  13(b)(5) of the Exchange Act [15 U.S.C. § 78m(b)(5)] and Rules 13b2-1 and 13b2-2 
18  promulgated thereunder [17 C.F.R. §§ 240.13b2-1 and 240.13b2-2] by: 
19         (a)	    knowingly circumventing or knowingly failing to implement a system of internal 
20                 accounting controls, or knowingly falsifying any book, record or account 
21                 described in Section 13(b)(2) of the Exchange Act [15 U.S.C. § 78m(b)(2)]; or 
22         (b)	    directly or indirectly falsifying or causing to be falsified any book, record or 
23                 account subject to Section 13(b)(2)(A) of the Exchange Act [15 U.S.C. § 
24                 78m(b)(2)(A)]; or 
25         (c)	    directly or indirectly making or causing to be made a materially false or 
26                 misleading statement, or omitting to state or causing another person to omit to 
27                 state any material fact, to an accountant in connection with any audit, review or 
28                 examination of the financial statements of an issuer or company, or the

 9                                                 III. 
10         IT IS FURTHER ORDERED, ADJUDGED, AND DECREED that Andrew D. Feldman 
11 and his agents, servants, employees, attorneys, and all persons in active concert or participation 
12  with him who receive actual notice of this Order of Permanent Injunction by personal service or 
13  otherwise are permanently restrained and enjoined from aiding and abetting any violation of 
14  Sections 13(a) and 13(b)(2)(A) of the Exchange Act [15 U.S.C. §§ 78m(a), 78m(b)(2)(A), and 
15  78m(b)(2)(B)(ii)] and Rules 12b-20, 13a-1, and 13a-13 thereunder [17 C.F.R. §§ 240.12b-20, 
16  240.13a-1, and 240.13a-13], by knowingly providing substantial assistance to an issuer that: 
17         (a)	    fails to file with the Commission such information, documents, or annual, 
18                 quarterly or periodic reports that the Commission may require or prescribe 
19                 pursuant to Section 13(a) of the Exchange Act or any of the rules, regulations or 
20                 forms promulgated thereunder; or 
21         (b)	    files with the Commission any information, document, or report, that contains any 
22                 untrue statement of a material fact, omits any material information, or otherwise 
23                 fails to comply with the provisions of Section 13(a) of the Exchange Act or any of 
24                 the rules, regulations or forms thereunder; or 
25         (c)	    fails to add or include, in addition to the information expressly required to be 
26                 included in a statement or report, such further material information as may be 
27                 necessary to make the required statements, in the light of the circumstances under 
28                 which they were made, not misleading; or

 4                                                IV. 
 5         IT IS FURTHER ORDERED, ADJUDGED, AND DECREED that Andrew D. Feldman 
 6  is liable for disgorgement of $289,507 (two hundred and eighty-nine thousand, five hundred and 
 7  seven dollars), representing profits gained as a result of the conduct alleged in the Complaint, 
 8  together with prejudgment interest thereon in the amount of $106,589 (one hundred and six 
 9  thousand, five hundred and eighty-nine dollars), for a total of $396,096 (three hundred and 
10  ninety-six thousand, ninety-six dollars).  Andrew D. Feldman shall satisfy this obligation by 
11 paying a total of $396,096 (three hundred and ninety-six thousand, ninety-six dollars) within ten 
12  (10) business days after the date of entry of this Order of Permanent Injunction by certified 
13  check, bank cashier’s check, United States postal money order or check drawn upon the trust 
14  account of Latham & Watkins, LLP payable to the Securities and Exchange Commission.  The 
15  payment shall be delivered or mailed to the Office of Financial Management, Securities and 
16  Exchange Commission, Operations Center, 6432 General Green Way, Mail Stop 0-3, Alexandria, 
17  Virginia 22312, together with a cover letter identifying Andrew D. Feldman as a defendant in this 
18  action, setting forth the title and civil action number of this action and the name of this Court, 
19  and specifying that payment is made pursuant to this Order of Permanent Injunction.  Andrew D. 
20  Feldman shall simultaneously transmit photocopies of such payment and the transmittal letter to 
21  the Securities and Exchange Commission’s attorney in this action.  The Securities and Exchange 
22  Commission shall remit the funds paid pursuant to this paragraph to the United States Treasury. 
23                                                V.

25  shall pay post-judgment interest on any delinquent amounts pursuant to 28 U.S.C. § 1961.
