 2                                                 I. 
 3         IT IS ORDERED, ADJUDGED AND DECREED, that L. John Kern and his agents, 
 4  servants, employees, attorneys, and all persons in active concert or participation with them who 
 5  receive actual notice of this Order of Permanent Injunction by personal service or otherwise are 
 6  permanently restrained and enjoined from violating, directly or indirectly, Section 10(b) of the 
 7  Securities Exchange Act of 1934 (the “Exchange Act”) [15 U.S.C. § 78j(b)] and Rule 10b-5 
 8  promulgated thereunder [17 C.F.R. § 240.10b-5], by using any means or instrumentality of 
 9  interstate commerce, or of the mails, or of any facility of any national securities exchange, in 
10  connection with the purchase or sale of any security: 
11         (a) 	   to employ any device, scheme, or artifice to defraud; 
12         (b) 	   to make any untrue statement of a material fact or to omit to state a material fact 
13                 necessary in order to make the statements made, in the light of the circumstances  
14                 under which they were made, not misleading; or 
15         (c)	    to engage in any act, practice, or course of business which operates or would 
16                 operate as a fraud or deceit upon any person. 
17                                                 II. 
18         IT IS FURTHER ORDERED, ADJUDGED, AND DECREED that L. John Kern and his

20  them who receive actual notice of this Order of Permanent Injunction by personal service or 
21  otherwise are permanently restrained and enjoined from violating, directly or indirectly, Section 
22  13(b)(5) of the Exchange Act [15 U.S.C. § 78m(b)(5)] and Rules 13b2-1 and 13b2-2 
23  promulgated thereunder [17 C.F.R. §§ 240.13b2-1 and 240.13b2-2] by: 
24         (a)	    knowingly circumventing or knowingly failing to implement a system of internal 
25                 accounting controls, or knowingly falsifying any book, record or account 
26                 described in Section 13(b)(2) of the Exchange Act [15 U.S.C. § 78m(b)(2)]; or

16                                                 III. 
17         IT IS FURTHER ORDERED, ADJUDGED, AND DECREED that L. John Kern and his 
18  agents, servants, employees, attorneys, and all persons in active concert or participation with 
19  them who receive actual notice of this Order of Permanent Injunction by personal service or 
20  otherwise are permanently restrained and enjoined from aiding and abetting any violation of 
21  Sections 13(a) and 13(b)(2)(A) of the Exchange Act [15 U.S.C. §§ 78m(a) and 78m(b)(2)(A)] 
22  and Rules 12b-20, 13a-1, and 13a-13 thereunder [17 C.F.R. §§ 240.12b-20, 240.13a-1, and 
23  240.13a-13], by knowingly providing substantial assistance to an issuer that: 
24         (a)	    fails to file with the Commission such information, documents, or annual, 
25                 quarterly or periodic reports that the Commission may require or prescribe 
26                 pursuant to Section 13(a) of the Exchange Act or any of the rules, regulations or 
27                 forms promulgated thereunder; or

14  for disgorgement of $347,066 (three hundred and forty-seven thousand and sixty-six dollars),

16  prejudgment interest thereon in the amount of $125,430 (one hundred twenty-five thousand, four 
17  hundred and thirty dollars), for a total of $472,496 (four hundred and seventy-two thousand, four 
18  hundred and ninety six dollars).  L. John Kern shall satisfy this obligation within ten business 
19  days after the date of entry of this Order of Permanent Injunction by the Clerk of this Court, by 
20  paying $472,496 (four hundred and seventy-two thousand, four hundred and ninety six dollars) 
21  by certified check, bank cashier’s check, or United States postal money order payable to the 
22  Securities and Exchange Commission.  The payment shall be delivered or mailed to the Office of 
23  Financial Management, Securities and Exchange Commission, Operations Center, 6432 General 
24  Green Way, Mail Stop 0-3, Alexandria, Virginia 22312, together with a cover letter identifying L. 
25  John Kern as a defendant in this action, setting forth the title and civil action number of this 
26  action and the name of this Court, and specifying that payment is made pursuant to this Order of 
27  Permanent Injunction.  L. John Kern shall simultaneously transmit photocopies of such payment
