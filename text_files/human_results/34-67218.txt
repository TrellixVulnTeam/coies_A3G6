                                            I. 
 
      The Securities and Exchange Commission deems it appropriate to issue an order of 
forthwith suspension of Laurence M. Brown (Brown) pursuant to Rule 102(e)(2) of the 
Commissions Rules of Practice, 17 C.F.R. § 201.102(e)(2) .1
                                                         
 
                                            II. 
       
      The Commission finds that:  
 
      1.     Brown, age 64, is and has been a certified public accountant licensed to practice in 
the State of New York.  From at least <date.>January 1, 2008</> until <date.>June 30, 2010</>, Brown was a principal, 
and managing and general partner of <comp.>Marshall Granger & Company LLP</>, an accounting firm 
located in Armonk, New York. 
 
      2.     On January 25, 2012 , a judgment of conviction was entered against Brown in 
United States v. Brown               , No. 10-Cr. 991 (MGC), in the United States District Court for the 
Southern District of New York, finding him guilty of <item.>one c ount of securities fraud</> in violation of 
15 U.S.C. § 78j(b), two counts of wire fraud in violation of 18 U.S.C. § 1342, and one count of

       3.     As a result of this conviction, Brown was sentenced to 60 months imprisonment

                                             III. 
 
       In view of the foregoing, the Commission finds that Brown  has been convicted of a
